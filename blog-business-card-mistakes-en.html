5 Common Business Card Design Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Information Overload

Mistake: Trying to put every piece of information in a small space leads to a cluttered and hard-to-read card.

Solution: Simplicity and focusing on essential information, using a single link as a gateway to more opportunities.

Poor Design and Print Quality

Mistake: Using an outdated design or low-quality printing makes the card look cheap and unprofessional.

Solution: Use professional ready-made templates, and rely on digital cards which always look crisp and clear.

No Clear Call to Action

Mistake: Merely listing contact info without guiding the client on what to do next.

Solution: Use links that direct the user to browse your portfolio, make a booking, or contact you directly.

Using Outdated Information

Mistake: Distributing cards with old information damages credibility.

Solution: Update data in real-time via a digital card to ensure information accuracy.

Ignoring the Power of QR Code & NFC

Mistake: Relying on manual data entry, which reduces the likelihood of saving your information.

Solution: Provide a QR Code and NFC tools to make saving contact details easy with a single click.
